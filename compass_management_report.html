What is the Compass?

The Compass is a 10-question survey that every new member completes when they sign up. It takes roughly 3 minutes.

At the end, the platform assigns each member an Eprenz Score (0–1,000) and a Stage. Everything else in the app — their personalised path, partner matches, Atlas AI advice, and goals — is built on this score.

Members cannot access the main dashboard until the Compass is complete. If they leave mid-survey, their progress is saved and they resume from where they left off on next login.

The 10 questions

Q1
Business stage
Self-perception only — no score points. Used as a tiebreaker.
Q2
How many businesses do you own?
Measures founder experience. Up to 50 pts.
Q3
Annual revenue
Biggest single lever. Up to 340 pts across two dimensions.
Q4
Team size
Measures ability to delegate and scale. Up to 200 pts.
Q5
Biggest challenge right now
Tags used for partner and advisor matching downstream.
Q6 optional
Cost of that challenge
Execution awareness signal. Up to 50 pts.
Q7 optional
Support areas wanted
Multi-select. Shapes recommendations and adds small bonus pts per selection.
Q8
Invested in business last 90 days?
Strongest execution signal. Up to 200 pts.
Q9 optional
Open to partner matches?
Network openness signal. Up to 15 pts.
Q10
Monthly investment capacity
Closes the profile. Up to 280 pts across two dimensions.

Optional questions (Q6, Q7, Q9) show a "Skip" button. Answering them can add meaningful points — particularly for borderline stage decisions — but skipping doesn't block the member from proceeding.

The 4 stages

Start 0–249

Idea stage through first revenue. Focus on getting customers and proving the model.

Grow 250–499

Selling consistently. Building systems and team. Focus on scaling what works.

Profit 500–749

Profitable with strong systems. Focus on margins, cash flow, and strategic moves.

Exit 750–1,000

Building toward exit or succession. Focus on business value and transition planning.

One important rule: A member's self-perception (Q1) can pull their assigned stage down by at most one step — it can never push it higher. If the data says Grow but the member says Start, they land in Start. If the data says Profit but the member says Start, they still land in Grow. This prevents gaming the survey.
